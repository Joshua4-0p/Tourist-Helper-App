Health

The CDC and WHO recommend the following vaccination for the cameroon: hepatitis A, hepatitis B, typhoid, cholera, yellow fever, robies, meningitis, polio, measles, mumps and rubelia (MMR), Tdap (tetanus diphtheria and pertussia), chickenpox, shingles, pneumonia and influenza.

Pharmacies in Cameroon have a green cross outside. Most are extremely helpful to clients. Most facilities will require cash payments before medical services are administered

Insect

Mosquitoes, ticks and fleas can transmit a number of diseases. Some of which can't be prevented with a vaccine or drugs. However, the risk can be reduced by taking certain precautions:

  • Cover the skin
  • Use a suitable repellent
  • Sleep in air-conditioned
  • If you sleep outside use a mosquito
  • Use Permethrin to treat clothing and Permethrin

Animal

Most animals avoid humans, but can bite or scratch if they feel threatened

  • Do not feed or touch strange animals
  • Keep animals away from open wounds
  • Avoid animal saliva
  • Avoid rodents

Weather

Throughout the year, Buea displays moderatley high temperature with little variation, looking at the average highs and lows, there's a narrow rangefrom 27.2*C to 32.1*C for the highs, and from 23.2*C to 18.3*C for the lows. Regarding humidity, the city faces pretty high and consistent humidity levels year-round. The city records heavy rainfall especially in the months from May to November and consistent sunshine throughout the rest of the months

Taking into account the overall weather conditions, the best time to visit Buea would most probably be from December to Febraury. The temperature are within a pleasant range, with highs, flunctuating from 30.8*C to 32.1*C. Thus, these months provide an excellent opportunity for visitors who plans to take part in outdoor activities without worrying about heavy showers

Local Law

  • Always carry proof of your identity. This can be your resident permit or a certified copy of your passport
  • Same-sex relationship are illegal, penalties include 6 months to 5 years jail and a fine of 20,000 to 200,000 francs
  • Be careful when taking photos. it's illegal to photograph military zones, assets or personnels. Taking photos of government buildings, airports and ports is also illegal
  • Dress and behaviour standards are conservative. Always dress appropriately.

Security Advice

General strikes (or 'ghost town') are called in the northwest and southwest(Anglophone) regions for each Monday, with additional days often called in particular periods including Febraury (around Youth Day, 11 Febraury), May (around National Day on 20 May) and October(arround 1 October). Violence and travel disruption is regularly reported on these days
